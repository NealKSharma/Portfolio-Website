My computer science journey started back in eighth grade with a simple question: “How can I mod Minecraft?” That curiosity led me to learn Java, and before long, I wasn’t just playing games — I was creating inside them. Ever since then, computers have felt like home. From playing Road Rash on a chunky old monitor with a ball mouse to accidentally breaking my grandfather’s PC while trying to upgrade its RAM, every experience just pulled me deeper into this world. I even taught myself web development long before it showed up in any class, not because I had to, but because I genuinely loved figuring things out.

That excitement hasn’t faded — if anything, it’s grown. Coding has always been a passion for me, and it’s something I want to stick with for the long run. I’m not completely sure which direction I’ll go yet, but I know my interests lie somewhere between web development, software engineering, and artificial intelligence. Whether it’s building systems that people actually use, researching AI safety, or just exploring new technologies, what drives me is the challenge of turning ideas into real, working solutions that make a difference.
